Work

For this case study I analyzed data given from Lariat, a car rental agency, to make better decisions about vehicles they purchase for their car rental fleet. I identified 3 potential strategies to better improve their overall profitability, by minimizing costs and maximizing revenue. I also created two visual interactive dashboards comparing the revenue, costs and profit per branch (Branch Performance) as well as monthly costs, number of times rented and average rental length per vehicle (Vehicle Performance) to give a better understanding of the most and least profitable branches and vehicles within the fleet.

For this case study I analyzed data pulled from Kaggle.com, which contained wine data reviews from various locations around the world including variety, price, points, regions, and descriptions of each wine. The purpose of this data analysis is to give insight on statistical findings and hypotheses regarding based on a dependent variable within the dataset. Two hypotheses were tested within my analysis which included creating a scatter plot, testing the Pearson Coefficient, conducting a T-Test, and creating margin charts to either reject or fail to reject my null hypotheses. The results of the analysis could be beneficial for potential wine sellers and or marketers as they conduct sales and presentations to specific target demographics and buyers.
